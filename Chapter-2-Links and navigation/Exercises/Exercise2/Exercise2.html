The Solar System

The Solar System is a group of celestial objects that includes the Sun, eight planets, their moons, and various other bodies like asteroids and comets. It is located in the Milky Way galaxy and has fascinated scientists for centuries.

Learning about the Solar System helps us understand how Earth fits into the universe and how other worlds compare to our own.

Formation

  • The Solar System formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a cloud of gas and dust.
  • Gravity pulled the materials together to form the Sun and planets.
  • The leftover material became moons, asteroids, and comets.

Fun Facts

  • Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System.
  • Venus spins in the opposite direction to most planets.
  • Neptune has the strongest winds recorded in the Solar System.
  • Earth is the only planet known to support life.
